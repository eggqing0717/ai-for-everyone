# AI for Everyone

> **Helping every ordinary person truly understand how AI works under the hood.**

*AI for Everyone* is an AI literacy guide written for the general public. We believe that AI shouldn't be a secret language spoken only by engineers—anyone curious about the world deserves to genuinely **understand** how it thinks.

---

## What Makes This Book Different

- **🌱 Beginner-friendly**: No advanced math, no coding background required. If you can use a smartphone, you can read this.
- **🎯 Metaphors first, plain talk always**: We take intimidating terms like "gradient descent," "attention mechanism," and "Transformer" and turn them into stories from everyday life.
- **🖼️ Visual-first**: Every abstract concept comes with an intuitive picture, so you can "get it at a glance."
- **🧭 Step by step**: From "what exactly is AI?" all the way to how GPT works—a clear path, with no steps skipped.

## Who This Book Is For

- Curious readers who keep getting scared off by the jargon
- Anyone who wants to explain AI clearly to their kids, family, or colleagues
- Professionals in non-technical roles who still need to understand what AI is doing
- Everyone who believes that "even complicated things can be explained simply"

## Table of Contents at a Glance (Six Parts)

1. **A Worldview of AI Basics** — What AI is, where it came from, and how it's already all around you.
2. **Foundations of Machine Learning** — Data, models, training, and evaluation: four steps to understanding how machines "learn."
3. **Neural Networks and Deep Learning** — From the inspiration of the brain to CNNs and RNNs: how machines make sense of images and language.
4. **From Word Embeddings to the Transformer** — The cornerstone of large models: the attention mechanism and the Transformer architecture.
5. **Large Language Models in Practice** — Prompts, fine-tuning, RAG, and the limits and future of AI.
6. **Appendix** — A quick-reference glossary, learning resources, visualization tools, and an AI timeline.
