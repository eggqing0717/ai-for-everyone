# Part 3: Neural Networks & Deep Learning · Part Overview

Hi, I'm Jay. Welcome to the most "hardcore"—and most exciting—part of this book.

If the first two parts were about "what AI is" and "how machines learn," then from here on we're going to actually pop the hood on today's AI and take a look at the roaring engine inside: the **neural network**. Every buzzword you hear today—ChatGPT, facial recognition, self-driving cars, AI art—can be traced back, almost without exception, to this one technology.

Don't worry, we'll still keep it in plain language. I promise that by the end of this part, you'll have a crystal-clear intuition for "what exactly makes deep learning 'deep,' how machines learn to see pictures, and how they learn to read a sentence."

## What We'll Cover in This Part

- **Chapter 9 · Neural Networks: Inspiration from the Brain**—It turns out this technology really did "copy the homework" from our own brains. We'll start with the smallest building block: a single artificial neuron.
- **Chapter 10 · How Neural Networks Learn: Backpropagation**—What happens when the network gets an answer wrong? Like a detective, it works backward from the outcome, assigns blame layer by layer, and then corrects itself.
- **Chapter 11 · Why Deep Learning Is "Deep"**—"Deep" isn't mysticism; it's about letting machines understand the world layer by layer, from coarse to fine.
- **Chapter 12 · Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs)**—How does a machine "see" an image? With a sliding "magnifying glass."
- **Chapter 13 · Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs)**—To handle information with an inherent order, like text and speech, a machine needs a bit of "memory."
- **Chapter 14 · A Quick Tour of Common Deep Learning Models**—A single comparison table to help you get acquainted with the "famous schools" like ResNet and YOLO.

## Three Small Tips for You

1. **Don't be scared by the word "neural."** It sounds like biology class, but at its core it's just "multiply, add, and then make a decision"—elementary-school math is plenty.
2. **Heavy on analogies, light on formulas.** I write almost no formulas in this part. For every tricky point, I'll first paint a scene from everyday life, then reveal its technical name.
3. **Read in order.** Chapters 9 and 10 are the foundation; the CNNs and RNNs that follow are buildings raised on top of it. Once the foundation is solid, the rest will feel easy.

Ready? We'll start from the smallest building block—"a single neuron"—and gradually assemble the entire AI skyscraper. Turn the page, let's go!
