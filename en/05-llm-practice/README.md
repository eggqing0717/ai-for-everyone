# Part Five: Large Language Models in Practice · Part Overview

After all that theory, we've finally reached the most exciting step—**actually putting AI to work**.

In the first four parts, we were "learning how a car is built": how the engine works, how the transmission transfers power. In this part, we're getting behind the wheel and **learning to drive**. You don't need to know every component of the engine to drive smoothly and well. Likewise, you don't need to know how to train a large model to use AI like a pro.

## What We'll Cover

The goal of this part is refreshingly practical: **by the time you finish reading, you'll be able to use AI hands-on**. Whether you want AI to help you write copy, organize information, answer questions, or you simply want to understand how those "AI customer-service bots" and "intelligent knowledge bases" actually work—you'll find the answers here.

We'll make four stops:

| Chapter | Topic | In One Sentence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Chapter 20 | Prompt Engineering | Learn to "talk right" so AI understands what you really want |
| Chapter 21 | Pre-training, Fine-tuning & Transfer Learning | How AI goes to "college" and then "grad school" to specialize |
| Chapter 22 | RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) | Give AI a "reference library" so it stops making things up |
| Chapter 23 | Limitations, Ethics & the Future | Recognize AI's boundaries and take a clear-eyed view of what it can and can't do |

## How to Read This Part

- **Chapter 20 is the most hands-on—strongly recommended to try it yourself.** Prompt techniques are a skill anyone can use immediately. After reading, open an AI tool and practice along.
- **Chapters 21 and 22 help you "understand what's really going on."** Even if you never do it yourself, understanding fine-tuning and RAG will show you why some AI tools are especially good at your industry.
- **Chapter 23 helps you "stay clear-headed."** AI is powerful, but it isn't omnipotent. Knowing where it can go wrong lets you use it more safely and with greater confidence.

> 💡 **Tip**: This part involves almost no math formulas—it's all practical, actionable content. Even if you skipped the earlier theory chapters, you can start right here and benefit immediately.

Ready? Let's begin with "how to communicate effectively with AI."
