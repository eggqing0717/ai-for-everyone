# Appendix · Section Guide

Congratulations! By reaching this point, you've followed this book through the complete journey from "What is AI" to "How to use large models." This appendix isn't meant to be read "cover to cover." Instead, it's a handy **toolbox** you can flip open anytime—when you forget a term, want to find learning materials, or feel like getting hands-on with AI, this is the place to turn to.

> In one sentence: **the main text is the textbook; the appendix is your pocket reference manual.**

## How to Use This Appendix

- **Quick reference first, no memorization needed.** The glossary is like a dictionary—look things up when you need to, and you'll naturally remember them after a few lookups.
- **Consult as needed, jump around freely.** Each appendix is relatively self-contained, so you can start reading from any section.
- **Doing beats reading.** We strongly recommend spending 10 minutes opening any of the visualization sites in [Appendix C](03-tools.md) to see for yourself what "a neural network learning" and "attention focusing" actually look like—it's far more intuitive than reading ten pages of text.
- **Don't let English scare you.** For each resource in the appendix, we've noted whether it's "Chinese-friendly" and whether it's "free," so even complete beginners can find something that suits them.

## A Small Reminder

The AI field moves incredibly fast. The specific products, versions, and figures mentioned in this book may change over time, but **the underlying principles are stable.** So:

- Remember the **concepts and ways of thinking**, not the exact parameters of some model.
- When you encounter a new term or product, use the framework you've learned in this book to understand it—you'll find that "no matter how it changes, the essence stays the same."

Ready? Treat this appendix as your AI learning "memo," to be consulted whenever you need it. May you go ever further in the world of AI! 🚀
