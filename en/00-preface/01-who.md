# Who This Book Is For

> Before buying a book, we all like to flip through it to see whether it's really a fit. This section will help you run a quick three-minute "self-check."

## 1. A Quick Self-Test

Take a look at the statements below and see if any of them sound like you:

- [ ] I hear "AI," "large models," and "ChatGPT" all the time, but I've never truly understood what they are.
- [ ] I've used a few AI products, but I have no idea how they work behind the scenes.
- [ ] I gave my math back to my teachers long ago—one look at a formula and my head spins.
- [ ] I'm not a programmer, and I have no plans to learn to code.
- [ ] I'm a little worried AI might take my job, but I can't quite pin down what exactly to worry about.
- [ ] I'm just plain curious: how on earth does this thing "get smart"?

**If you checked even one of these, this book was written for you.**

## 2. Three Types of Readers—Find Yourself

While writing, we kept three friends in mind. See which one you resemble most.

### Reader A: The Curious "Outsider"

You might be a humanities major, a retired parent or grandparent, or anyone with no connection to tech. You don't care how the code is written; you just want to know: "What *is* AI, in plain terms? How can it understand what I say, draw pictures, and write essays?"

**What this book gives you**: using everyday metaphors—grocery shopping, cooking, teaching a child—it lays out the whole story of AI clearly, so you can hold your own in a dinner-table conversation.

### Reader B: The Hands-On "Doer" Who Wants to Use AI Well

You're an office worker, a teacher, a content creator, an entrepreneur… You already use AI to help write proposals, make slide decks, and brainstorm ideas, but you always feel you're "not quite hitting the mark," and sometimes it makes things up with a straight face.

**What this book gives you**: it helps you get a feel for AI's "personality"—what it's good at, where it goes off the rails, and why. Once you understand the principles, you'll know how to make it work for you more smoothly.

### Reader C: The "Student" Ready to Get Started

You're a high schooler, a college student, or someone looking to switch careers or level up. You plan to head toward AI in the future, but you're still missing a non-intimidating starting point.

**What this book gives you**: a clear "map." It won't hurl formulas at you from the get-go. Instead, it first helps you see the whole forest clearly, laying a solid foundation for the more specialized textbooks and courses you'll tackle later.

## 3. Who This Book Is **Not** For

Let's be honest and talk about its limits, too, so you don't buy the wrong book:

- **Engineers who want to write AI code and run models right away**: there's almost no code or math here, so you may find it too shallow.
- **Researchers looking for cutting-edge paper breakdowns**: this book covers the "foundation-laying" basics and doesn't chase the details of the latest papers.
- **People after a "master it in three days, earn a fortune in a month" shortcut**: sorry, there's only solid understanding here, no overnight-riches shortcut.

If you fall into one of these groups, you can treat this book as a "warm-up read"—working through it first will make the harder material go down much more smoothly.

## 4. Before You Start, You Only Need Three Things

1. **A dose of curiosity**—a willingness to ask "why."
2. **A bit of patience**—some concepts take a second read to click, and that's perfectly normal.
3. **Zero prerequisites**—you really don't need any math or programming background.

It's that simple.

## Chapter Summary

- As long as you're curious about AI and don't want to be scared off by formulas, this book is for you.
- We wrote for three types of readers: the curious outsider, the hands-on doer who wants to use AI well, and the student ready to get started.
- It's not for those chasing code, chasing papers, or chasing shortcuts.
- The bar for reading this book is just three things: curiosity, patience, and zero prerequisites.
