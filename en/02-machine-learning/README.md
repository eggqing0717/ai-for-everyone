# Part Two: The Basics of Machine Learning · Part Overview

> In the last part we talked about "what AI actually is," and learned that it's really a field devoted to making machines smart. In this part, we'll uncover its most central secret: how machines actually **"learn" things**.

## Let's start in plain language

Have you ever wondered: why can your phone's photo album automatically recognize your cat? Why can navigation apps predict which road will get jammed? Why do shopping websites seem to know what you want to buy better than you do yourself?

None of these abilities were **hand-coded by programmers, rule by rule**. Almost all of them come from the same approach—**Machine Learning**.

Machine learning is the "engine" behind the vast majority of today's AI. Those impressive-sounding terms you'll hear later—neural networks, deep learning, large models, ChatGPT—are, at their core, all branches growing from the great tree of machine learning. So **this part is the foundation of the whole book**. Once the foundation is solid, everything that follows gets easier.

## What does machine learning actually "learn"?

Let's start with a down-to-earth definition:

**Machine learning is about letting machines figure out the patterns on their own from a large number of examples, and then using those patterns to handle brand-new situations they've never seen before.**

Here's an example: suppose you want to teach a child what a "cat" is. You wouldn't hand them a *Manual of Cat Definitions*. Instead, you'd point at one cat after another and say "this is a cat, and so is this." After seeing enough, the child can recognize a cat they've never seen on the street. What machine learning does is basically the same thing (this is just an analogy—reality is more complex).

## How to read this part

- **Don't be afraid of math.** In this part we try to avoid formulas. Where they're truly unavoidable, we'll first explain the idea in plain language, then just mention its name.
- **Focus on getting the "feel."** You don't need to memorize any computational details. As long as you finish with a clear, intuitive picture of "how machines learn," this part has done its job.
- **Analogies are just crutches.** The book uses lots of everyday analogies to help you understand, but please remember: the real technology is often far more complex than the analogy. Analogies are crutches to get you started, not the destination.

## Part summary

- Machine learning is the core method of today's AI; everything later in this book is built on top of it.
- Its essence is to **let machines figure out patterns from examples on their own**, and then apply them to new situations.
- This part revolves around four things—**data, model, training, and evaluation**—and by the end you'll have a complete, intuitive understanding of "how machines learn."
